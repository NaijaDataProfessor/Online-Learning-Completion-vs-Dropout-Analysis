--- Total Courses Completed Per Platform 
SELECT platform, COUNT(*) AS completed_courses, ROUND(AVG(completionpercentage),2) AS average_completion_percentage
FROM dataset
WHERE completionstatus = "Completed"
GROUP BY platform
ORDER BY completed_courses DESC;

/*
QUERY RESULT INTERPRETATION: TOTAL COURSES COMPLETED PER PLATFORM WITH AVERAGE COMPLETION PERCENTAGE

The query provides both the number of completed courses and the average completion percentage for each platform:

- edX: 38 completed courses, average completion 58.84%
- Coursera: 33 completed courses, average completion 66.7%
- Skillshare: 29 completed courses, average completion 66.48%
- Udemy: 28 completed courses, average completion 60.21%
- YouTube: 28 completed courses, average completion 65.32%

Analytical Insights:

1. edX has the highest number of completed courses (38), but its average completion percentage (58.84%) is lower than Coursera, Skillshare, and YouTube. 
This suggests that while more learners attempt edX courses, completion is less consistent.

2. Coursera and Skillshare have slightly fewer completed courses but maintain the highest average completion percentages (66.7% and 66.48% respectively), 
indicating strong engagement and effective course structure.

3. Udemy and YouTube have similar completed course counts (28), with YouTube showing a slightly higher average completion rate (65.32% vs 60.21%), 
reflecting more targeted or concise learning content that maintains engagement.

4. Overall, platforms with structured guidance, incentives, and interactive learning content (Coursera, Skillshare, YouTube) tend to achieve higher average completion percentages, 
even if the absolute number of completed courses is lower.

Business Insight:
Combining completion counts with average completion percentages provides a comprehensive understanding of learner engagement. 
Platforms can use these insights to balance course accessibility, content quality, and learner support to maximize both enrollment and completion effectiveness.
*/



--- Most Common Dropout Reasons 
SELECT dropoutreason AS dropout_reasons, COUNT(dropoutreason) AS count 
FROM dataset
WHERE dropoutreason <> "No Dropout"
GROUP BY dropout_reasons
ORDER BY count DESC;

/*
QUERY RESULT INTERPRETATION: MOST COMMON DROPOUT REASONS

The query identifies the primary reasons learners dropped out of courses:

- Time Constraint: 47 learners
- Too Difficult: 47 learners
- Lost Interest: 35 learners

Analytical Insights:

1. Time Constraint and Course Difficulty are tied as the top reasons
   for dropout, indicating that many learners struggle to allocate
   enough time or face challenges with the course material.

2. Loss of Interest accounts for a significant portion of dropouts,
   highlighting the importance of engaging content, interactive
   materials, and motivation mechanisms.

3. Understanding these dropout patterns helps platforms:
   - Adjust course length and pacing
   - Offer flexible learning schedules
   - Improve content design to maintain learner interest

Business Insight:
Addressing the top dropout reasons can increase course completion
rates, enhance learner satisfaction, and improve overall platform
retention.
*/


--- Number of User who lost Interest Per Platforms
SELECT platform, COUNT(userid) user_count
FROM dataset 
WHERE dropoutreason = "Lost Interest"
GROUP BY platform
ORDER BY user_count DESC;

--- Average Course Completion Percentage VS Experience Level 
SELECT experiencelevel AS experience_level, ROUND(AVG(completionpercentage),2) AS completion_percentage
FROM dataset
GROUP BY experience_level
ORDER BY completion_percentage DESC;

/*
QUERY RESULT INTERPRETATION: AVERAGE COURSE COMPLETION PERCENTAGE VS EXPERIENCE LEVEL

The query calculates the average completion percentage for learners grouped by their experience level:

- Working Professional: 51.97%
- Fresher: 50.67%
- Student: 48.62%

Analytical Insights:

1. Working Professionals have the highest average completion percentage,
   indicating higher motivation, discipline, or relevance of the courses
   to their career goals.

2. Freshers show slightly lower completion, possibly due to limited
   experience in managing self-paced learning or balancing multiple tasks.

3. Students have the lowest completion percentage, which may reflect
   competing academic commitments, lower engagement, or lack of
   immediate professional incentive.

4. Overall, learners with more experience tend to complete courses
   more effectively, highlighting the importance of aligning course
   content with learner motivation and career relevance.

Business Insight:
Tailoring course content and support based on learner experience
can improve completion rates, e.g., providing flexible pacing for
students and advanced, practical content for working professionals.
*/


--- Tech vs Non Tech Completion Comaprison
SELECT coursetype AS course_type, COUNT(*) AS completed_course, ROUND(AVG(completionpercentage),2) AS completion_rate
FROM dataset
WHERE completionstatus = "Completed"
GROUP BY course_type
ORDER BY completed_course DESC;


/*
QUERY RESULT INTERPRETATION: TECH VS NON-TECH COURSE COMPLETION

The query compares completed courses and average completion rates for Tech and Non-Tech courses:

- Non-Tech: 96 completed courses, average completion rate 66.19%
- Tech: 60 completed courses, average completion rate 58.77%

Analytical Insights:

1. Non-Tech courses have both higher completion counts and higher
   average completion rates, suggesting that learners find them
   more engaging or easier to complete.

2. Tech courses show lower completion rates and counts, which may
   be due to higher complexity, technical challenges, or prerequisite
   knowledge requirements.

3. This highlights the importance of:
   - Providing additional support or resources for Tech learners
   - Structuring Tech courses to enhance accessibility and motivation
   - Balancing course difficulty to reduce dropout rates

Business Insight:
Understanding the difference in completion between Tech and Non-Tech
courses can guide content strategy, learner support initiatives, and
resource allocation to improve overall course completion.
*/
